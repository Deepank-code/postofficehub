<p>The <strong>National Savings Certificate (NSC)</strong> is a <strong>government-backed fixed-income savings scheme</strong> that offers <strong>guaranteed returns with tax benefits under Section 80C</strong>. Ideal for low-risk investors, NSC is a popular choice for secure long-term savings.</p>

<h3>Key Highlights</h3>
<p>‚úÖ <strong>7.7% interest compounded annually</strong> (but payable at maturity)<br>
‚úÖ Minimum deposit: ‚Çπ1000; No maximum limit<br>

<h3>Latest Interest Rate (From 01 Jan 2024)</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>7.7% per annum (compounded annually, payable on maturity)</strong></li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h3>Salient Features</h3>
<h4>(a) Who Can Open:</h4>
<ul>
<li>A single adult (resident Indian)</li>
<li>Joint account (up to 3 adults)</li>
<li>Guardian on behalf of a minor or a person of unsound mind</li>
<li>A minor above 10 years in their own name</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h4>(b) Deposits:</h4>
<ul>
<li>Minimum deposit: ‚Çπ1000</li>
<li>Subsequent deposits in multiples of ‚Çπ100</li>
<li>No maximum investment limit</li>
<li>Any number of accounts can be opened</li>
<li>Investments qualify for <strong>deduction under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act</strong></li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h4>(c) Maturity:</h4>
<ul>
<li>The NSC matures after <strong>5 years</strong> from the date of deposit</li>
<li>Interest is compounded annually and <strong>paid as a lump sum</strong> at maturity</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h4>(d) Pledging as Security:</h4>
<p>NSC certificates can be pledged or transferred as security by submitting a prescribed application and acceptance letter from the pledgee.</p>
<p>Permitted institutions for pledging include:</p>
<ul>
<li>The President of India or State Governor</li>
<li>RBI, Scheduled Banks, Co-operative Banks or Societies</li>
<li>Public/Private Corporations, Government Companies, Local Authorities</li>
<li>Housing Finance Companies</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h4>(e) Premature Closure:</h4>
<p>NSC cannot be closed before 5 years <strong>except</strong> under the following circumstances:</p>
<ul>
<li>Death of the sole account holder or any/all holders in a joint account</li>
<li>Forfeiture by a pledgee (e.g. a Gazetted officer)</li>
<li>On the order of a court</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h4>(f) Transfer of Account:</h4>
<p>The account/certificate can be transferred to another person in specific cases:</p>
<ul>
<li>Upon the death of the account holder, to the nominee or legal heirs</li>
<li>From the deceased account holder to surviving joint holder(s)</li>
<li>On court order</li>
<li>When pledged to a qualifying authority</li>
</ul>
