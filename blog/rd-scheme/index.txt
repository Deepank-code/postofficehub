1b:T133f,<p>The <strong>Post Office Recurring Deposit (RD) Scheme</strong> is one of the most trusted saving plans in India, loved by millions for decades. It’s designed especially for individuals who wish to build a secure fund by depositing small amounts every month, while earning a steady interest.</p>
<p>Currently, the Post Office RD offers an attractive <strong>interest rate of 6.7% per annum</strong>, compounded quarterly. The minimum amount you can deposit is <strong>₹100 per month</strong>, or any amount in multiples of ₹10. There is no upper limit, making it flexible for all income levels.</p>
<h2>Who Can Open an RD Account?</h2>
<p>An RD account at the Post Office can be opened by:</p>
<ul>
<li>A single adult.</li>
<li>Joint account holders (up to three adults, either Joint A or Joint B).</li>
<li>A guardian on behalf of a minor.</li>
<li>A guardian for a person of unsound mind.</li>
<li>A minor above 10 years of age, in their own name.</li>
</ul>
<p>You can open <strong>multiple RD accounts</strong> if you wish.</p>
<h2>How to Make Deposits</h2>
<ul>
<li>You can open an RD account through cash or cheque. For cheques, the date of deposit will be the date the cheque is cleared.</li>
<li>The <strong>minimum monthly deposit</strong> is ₹100, and you can increase this in multiples of ₹10.</li>
<li>If you open the account on or before the 15th of the month, subsequent deposits should be made by the 15th of every month.</li>
<li>If you open the account after the 15th, deposits must be made by the last working day of each month.</li>
</ul>
<h2>What Happens in Case of Default?</h2>
<ul>
<li>If you miss a monthly deposit, a default fee applies — ₹1 for every ₹100 denomination (proportional for other amounts).</li>
<li>To continue, you must first pay the missed installment plus the default fee, then pay the current month’s deposit.</li>
<li>If you miss 4 consecutive deposits, your account becomes <strong>discontinued</strong>, but you can revive it within 2 months from the fourth default. After that, no further deposits are allowed if not revived.</li>
<li>If there are up to 4 missed deposits, you may extend the maturity period by the number of missed months and pay the pending installments during that extended period.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Advance Deposit Option</h2>
<ul>
<li>You can pay your RD installments in advance for up to 5 years.</li>
<li>A rebate is available for advance deposits — for a ₹100 denomination, you get a rebate of ₹10 for 6 advance installments or ₹40 for 12 installments.</li>
<li>Advance deposits can be made at the time of opening or anytime later.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Loan Facility</h2>
<ul>
<li>After you deposit 12 installments and the account runs for at least 1 year (without discontinuation), you can avail a <strong>loan up to 50% of the balance</strong>.</li>
<li>The loan can be repaid in a lump sum or in monthly installments.</li>
<li>The loan interest rate is <strong>2% higher than the RD interest rate</strong> and is calculated from the withdrawal date to the repayment date.</li>
<li>If you do not repay the loan before maturity, the loan plus interest will be deducted from the maturity amount.</li>
<li>To apply, submit the loan application form along with your passbook at the Post Office.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Premature Closure</h2>
<ul>
<li>You can close your RD account <strong>after 3 years</strong> from the date of opening by submitting the prescribed form.</li>
<li>If you close the account prematurely, you will receive interest as per the Post Office Savings Account rate — even if the account is closed just one day before maturity.</li>
<li>Premature closure is not allowed during the period for which advance deposits have been made.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Maturity &#x26; Extension</h2>
<ul>
<li>The standard RD account matures after <strong>5 years</strong> (60 monthly deposits).</li>
<li>You can extend the account for another 5 years at the same interest rate applicable when the account was opened.</li>
<li>An extended account can be closed anytime during the extended period. For completed years, the RD rate applies; for a partial year, the Savings Account rate applies.</li>
<li>You can also keep the account for up to 5 years after maturity without making further deposits.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Repayment in Case of Death</h2>
<ul>
<li>If the account holder passes away, the nominee or legal heirs can claim the balance by submitting the required documents at the Post Office.</li>
<li>The nominee or legal heirs may also continue the RD account till maturity by applying at the concerned Post Office.</li>
</ul>
