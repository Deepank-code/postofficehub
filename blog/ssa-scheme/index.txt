1b:T10de,<h2>Post Office Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSA)</h2>
<p>The <strong>Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSA)</strong> under the Post Office is a <strong>high-interest, government-backed savings scheme designed for the girl child</strong>, helping parents secure their daughter's education and future while enjoying tax-free returns and 80C benefits.</p>
<hr>
<h3>Key Highlights</h3>
<p>‚úÖ <strong>8.2% per annum (compounded yearly)</strong><br>
‚úÖ Tax-free interest under Income Tax Act<br>
‚úÖ Minimum deposit: ‚Çπ250 per year, maximum: ‚Çπ1.5 lakh per year<br>
‚úÖ Deposits in lump sum or flexible installments<br>
‚úÖ Safe, government-backed, long-term savings</p>
<hr>
<h3>Latest Interest Rate (From 01 Jan 2024)</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>8.2% per annum, compounded annually</strong></li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h3>Salient Features</h3>
<h4>(a) Who Can Open:</h4>
<ul>
<li>Guardian (parents/legal guardian) in the <strong>name of a girl child below 10 years</strong></li>
<li>Only <strong>one account per girl child</strong> across banks/post offices</li>
<li>Maximum of <strong>two accounts per family</strong> (more allowed in case of twins/triplets)</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h4>(b) Deposit:</h4>
<ul>
<li>Minimum deposit: ‚Çπ250 to open and maintain the account</li>
<li>Maximum deposit: ‚Çπ1.5 lakh per financial year (in multiples of ‚Çπ50)</li>
<li>Deposits can be made in lump sum or multiple installments, with <strong>no limit on the number of deposits</strong> in a month/year</li>
<li>Deposits can be made for <strong>15 years from the date of account opening</strong></li>
<li>Accounts qualify for <strong>Section 80C tax deduction</strong></li>
<li>If the minimum deposit is not made, the account will become defaulted but can be revived by paying the minimum deposit plus a penalty of ‚Çπ50 per defaulted year</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h4>(c) Interest:</h4>
<ul>
<li>Calculated monthly on the lowest balance between the 5th and last day of the month</li>
<li>Credited annually at the end of each financial year</li>
<li>Interest is <strong>tax-free under the Income Tax Act</strong></li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h4>(d) Operation of Account:</h4>
<ul>
<li>Operated by the guardian until the girl child attains <strong>18 years of age</strong></li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h4>(e) Withdrawal Facility:</h4>
<ul>
<li>Allowed after the girl child turns 18 years or passes the 10th standard</li>
<li>Up to <strong>50% of the balance</strong> at the end of the preceding financial year can be withdrawn</li>
<li>Withdrawals can be made in a lump sum or in up to <strong>one withdrawal per year</strong> for a maximum of five years</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h4>(f) Premature Closure:</h4>
<p>Permissible after <strong>5 years from account opening</strong> under specific conditions:</p>
<ul>
<li>Death of the account holder (interest at PO Savings Account rate applies from the date of death to payment)</li>
<li>Extreme compassionate grounds such as:
<ul>
<li>Life-threatening illness of the account holder</li>
<li>Death of the guardian</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Requires documentation and submission of a prescribed form at the post office</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h4>(g) Closure on Maturity:</h4>
<ul>
<li>The account matures after <strong>21 years from the date of account opening</strong></li>
<li>Alternatively, the account can be closed for marriage after the girl child attains 18 years of age, but:
<ul>
<li>Closure is allowed <strong>only within 1 month before or 3 months after the date of marriage</strong></li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
